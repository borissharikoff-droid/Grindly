Devlog — The Raids & Parties Update (v3.9.0)

This one took a while. I want to write an honest account of what went into this patch, because it's by far the most technically complex thing I've shipped for Grindly. If you follow the development at all, you've probably noticed the cadence slowed down over the last few weeks — and this is why.

How it started

The original guild system had a "guild raid" concept baked into it — essentially a shared progress bar that all members contributed to. Simple, serverless-friendly, easy to balance. Then I started thinking: what if raids were their own thing? What if you could go on a raid with friends, not just your guild? What if roles mattered — tank, healer, DPS — and every member had a daily action tied to their role?

That's a completely different system. And I ripped out the old one to build it properly.

The schema problem

Raids involve multiple interlocking tables: raids, raid_participants, raid_invites, raid_history, parties, party_members, party_invites. Each with Row Level Security policies in Supabase to make sure you can only see raids you're part of, only modify your own participant row, and so on.

What I didn't anticipate: circular RLS. A policy on raid_participants was querying raids to check membership, and a policy on raids was querying raid_participants to check the same thing. Infinite recursion. The DB would just... refuse. This took two separate commits to fully untangle, and it's the kind of bug that's invisible until you hit it in production with real data.

The fix was to flatten the dependency — one table becomes the authority, the other references it without a back-reference in the policy. Simple in retrospect, extremely annoying to discover.

The party system

Parties were supposed to be a quick addition. A slim HUD bar at the top of the app, a panel in Friends to invite people, a +5% XP buff. Two days of work, maybe three. It ended up touching almost every major system.

Tank: "Defend Today" — boss deals half damage to party HP that night.

Healer: "Heal Party" — consume food/items from inventory to restore party HP.

DPS: attack. Same as before, but now it matters more.

Making each role feel different without making any of them useless was genuinely hard. There were builds where Tank was obviously the meta and builds where Healer was dead weight (nobody stockpiles consumables). The current version feels balanced, but it's early.

Async raid design — the core tension

Grindly is an idle game about your real work hours. You don't sit and play it — it runs in the background. Raids had to respect that. So raids are async: the boss deals damage once per day, you submit your daily action whenever you have time. Faster tiers run 2 days, standard runs 5, epic runs 7.

The tricky part: "once per day" means you store the last tick date server-side and compute elapsed days lazily on fetch. If three days went by and nobody checked in, the system applies three days of damage at once. If party HP hits zero at any point, the raid fails retroactively.

Guild raids vs. party raids

Guild raids are collective progress goals — 300 mob kills + 200 crafts in two days, 2000 kills for the epic tier. Everyone contributes passively. You just play normally and the bar fills.

Party raids are active boss fights with daily roles. The boss has phases. You can fail — wipe, death screen, lose accumulated gold. Guild raids are passive and social. Party raids are active and risky.

What else is in this patch

  • Two new arena zones — Shadow Crypt (Zone 7, Necromancer Lord) and Celestial Spire (Zone 8, Storm Titan, 45% attack spread — hardest fight in the game).
  • Guild system overhaul — guild tax (0–15%), guild invites from friend profiles, role management (kick/promote/demote).
  • Marketplace 2.0 — price sparklines, floor price badges, deduplication, Quick List auto-fill at floor − 1g.
  • Weekly challenges — four goals/week (kill / craft / farm / cook) alongside daily bounties.
  • Navigation restructure — Quests moved out of Arena into Profile. Arena = fights.
  • Inventory item comparison — inspect gear, see ATK/HP/DEF/Regen delta vs equipped. Green = upgrade.
  • Home ambient bar — chips showing farm ready count and active craft/cook jobs.

On balancing

You can't balance a system you haven't shipped. I can model raid damage mathematically all day, but I don't know if the numbers are right until real players try it. The economy stuff — guild tax, marketplace floor pricing, bounty rewards — is intentionally conservative. I'd rather buff later than ship an exploit on day one.

What's next

Balance pass on mid-game zones 4–6. That's where most players stall. Also UI polish I deferred to ship this patch on time.

If you're playing — thank you. Grindly is a weird experiment (a productivity tracker that turned into an MMO-lite) and every bit of feedback helps.
