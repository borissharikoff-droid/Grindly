01 · Mission & Pillars

What Grindly is, and isn't.

The product turns invisible time into visible progress. Most productivity tools shame the user for falling off; Grindly rewards them for showing up. Every tracked second of real activity is XP toward one of 14 skills, mapped from window titles via a PowerShell tracker. Friends see your guild, drops, kills. Bosses gate progression. Loot has stats. Crafting is real.

01

RPG, not productivity-porn

Compact MMO/RPG aesthetic. Stats over decoration. Dense, readable, intentional. Inventory grids, level numbers, XP bars — the things that mattered in RuneScape, ported to the desktop.

02

Earned, not gamed

XP comes from real activity tracked at the OS level — not self-reported clicks. The first 8 skills are passive (your coding gives Developer XP). The rest (Warrior, Crafter, Farmer, Chef) require in-game effort.

03

Social by default

Friends, guilds, group chats, marketplace, leaderboard. Powered by Supabase. Status lines tell your guildmates what you're doing live: Coding in VS Code, Fighting in Arena.

02 · Logo & Mascot

The Purple Block.

The mascot is a square pixel-art character in #c084fc — the same purple that powers the grindly skill, the prestige Void tier, and the epic loot rarity. It's deliberately low-poly and slightly dumb. Friendly, not corporate. Always centered, always looking forward, never animated more than a slight bob.

on surface-1 · #1e2024
on brand gradient
Grindly
DO
  • Use the mascot at image-rendering: pixelated — it's pixel art, treat it as such
  • Keep clearspace ≥ the height of one mascot eye on every side
  • Drop-shadow with purple glow (rgba(192,132,252,0.45)) for hero placement
  • Pair with the wordmark in Rajdhani 700, gradient purple→accent
DON'T
  • Don't smooth the pixels. Don't redraw it in vector. It's a sprite.
  • Don't tint it. Purple is the brand color, full stop.
  • Don't put it on busy gradients except the official brand gradient
  • Don't add motion beyond a subtle bob — no spins, no bounces

08 · Voice & Tone

Friendly nerd. RPG-literate. Never corporate.

Grindly speaks like a guildmate, not a productivity coach. It celebrates real progress with real numbers. It uses RPG vocabulary — XP, level, drop, prestige, mob, raid — with zero ironic distance. It never lectures. It never shames a missed streak.

YES — say it like
  • "+1,247 XP · Developer hit Lv 47"
  • "Forest Wolf down. 24 gold + Wolf Pendant (epic)."
  • "Streak 17. Same time tomorrow?"
  • "Crafter Lv 30 unlocked: -5% craft time"
  • "Friend started a guild raid. Join?"
NO — never write like
  • "You earned a productivity score!" (no scores)
  • "Don't break your streak!" (no shaming)
  • "Maximize your output potential" (no SaaS speak)
  • "Unleash your inner productivity warrior" (no cosplay copy)
  • "You haven't been active in 3 days 😢" (no guilt-tripping)

08.5 · Motion principles

Motion has a job. Or it doesn't ship.

Animation in Grindly is purposeful: it earns XP a little dopamine, signals a level-up, or softens a state change. Never decorative. All durations under 400ms. All easing from a short, opinionated palette. Powered by Framer Motion via src/renderer/lib/motion.ts.

120ms · ease-out

Reactive

Hover, focus, click. The interaction confirms itself. Buttons glow. Tabs swap. Tooltips fade in.

240ms · ease-in-out

State change

Modals open, panels expand, page transitions. Long enough to read, short enough not to feel sluggish.

2000ms · pulse loop

Ambient game-glow

Mythical drops pulse forever. Active session ring. Boss low-HP warning. The game is alive even when you aren't moving.

spring · stiffness 400

Pop-in (loot drop)

Items fall into the inventory with a snappy spring. Slight overshoot. The bag is heavier than they expect.

~200ms · linear

Counter tick

XP numbers count up, gold counts up, level numbers flip. Always linear so they feel mechanical, not theatrical.

~3000ms · ease-out

Level-up burst

The only big moment. Particles, glow, sound, big number. Earned, not deployed for shipping a feature.

10 · Anti-patterns

The 10 ways to break Grindly.

Pattern violations that drift the brand. Every one of these has been caught, fixed, or banned in CLAUDE.md.

Don't ship any of these
  • Generic SaaS card grid — purple gradient hero + 3 feature cards. That's every AI-generated landing page. We're not them.
  • Centered everything. Hero copy centered, sections centered, every card centered. Asymmetry is intentional in this product.
  • Bubbly large border-radius. Default is 4px, max is 8px. 12px+ belongs in a different app.
  • Decorative blobs / floating circles / wavy SVG. If a section feels empty, fix the content, don't add decoration.
  • Emoji as design elements. Emoji are skill icons and reactions. They are not bullet points or section dividers.
  • Inline icons in colored circles. The "icon-in-pill + 2-line description" repeated 3x. Forbidden.
  • Body text below 13px. Density is fine. Unreadable is not. text-body is the floor.
  • Raw emoji for loot items. Always use LootVisual. Items have art, treat them as art.
  • New rarity colors. Five tiers, locked. Don't invent "ultra-rare" or "transcendent". Prestige handles the post-99 ceiling.
  • Light theme. Grindly is dark-only. The whole identity assumes #111214 at the bottom of the stack.
There is no level 100.
